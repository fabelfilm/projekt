1917. ADINE und BONI live their utopia on an airship above the Baltic sea. They compose music and create movies about their hero Alexander Kropotkin, a prince who became revolutionary. They are anxiously awaiting a mission task from their anarchist cell that is preparing for world revolution.

1/4

In a storm, Adine rescues a shipwrecked woman: CLÉO. The next morning, Cléo provokes Adine, who counters, and finally the three find themselves in a vivid dance. When Cléo is asleep, Adine and Boni find their mission in a black envelope. They delve into preparations for the coup that is scheduled for the next day. Meanwhile, they discuss what to do with Cléo. Boni wants to keep her in the ship whereas Adine wants to get rid of her./p> 2/4

Adine is attracted by Cléo and loses more and more control. In the game between the two, Cléo intensifies her teasing and provokes a brutal fight in the ocean.

3/4

On the street towards the site of their coup, Adine and Boni are passed by Cléo. A general, her father, welcomes Cléo on his château where the noblesse of Europe has arrived for the masked ball. Among the guests, we make out a certain prince: Peter Kropotkin.

4/4
